    • General

    Coraline is a 2009 movie that is notable for its animation technique, 3D stop motion, and its intiguing narrative. Based on the novel by Nel Gaiman in 2002, This cimnematographic masterpiece was recognized as the first work of Laika, a renowed studio, for its innovation and creativity. Distributed by Focus Features, the story follows: Coraline is an intrepid girl who moves to a new house and whose adventures begin when she finds a hidden door in her new house, leading to a parallel world where everything feels like paradise. But behind the shining smiles and the many treats hide dark secrets that will get her to fight for her real life.

    The direction of the movie was in the hands of Henry Selick, and by his side was the original author of the book, Neil Gaiman, who actively participated in the process of adaptation. This collaboration was part of the great success of the movie and how immersive it felt in relation to the book.

    The premiere of Coraline in American cinema took place on February 6, 2009, after its successful presentation at the Portland International Film Festival. The movie was acclaimed by critics and gained over 16.85 million dollars during its first screening week, putting itself in third place at the box office. At the time of screening, the movie had gained over 124 million dollars all around the world. Additionally, it was praised with many awards, like a Golden Globe, an Academy Award, and more.

    Plot Summary

    Coraline is a girl who moves to an old house called The Pink Palace Apartments in Ashland, Oregon, after living her whole life in Pontias, Michigan. Her parents worked from home designing a flower catalogue, but they were always busy. Because of this, Coraline finds herself bored and alone in her new home. But this would suddenly take an unexpected twist.

    In the midst of her loneliness, she discovers a secret door in her home that takes her to a parallel world, the "Otherworld", where her parents have buttons for eyes and are charmingly attentive to her. However, this mysterious world is not what it seems.

    With the help of her new friend, Wybie, Coraline discovers that the Otherworld's mother, the Beldam, has kidnapped her parents and other children in the past, stealing their souls and leaving them trapped. Terrified but brave, Coraline embarks on a dangerous mission to free the captive souls and defeat the Beldam.

    After a series of confrontations, Coraline returns to the real world and reunites with her parents, but the danger still persists. She, along with Wybie and other residents of the Pink Palace, decide to take action to prevent the threat of the Beldam from returning.

    Coraline is a captivating film that takes us on a journey through alternate worlds, exploring themes of courage, determination and the importance of family. Coraline shows that even in terrifying situations, bravery and determination can overcome any challenge.

    Production

    Coraline was filmed in a 13,000 square-meters warehouse in Hillsboro, Oregon. The stage was divided into fifty areas, which housed almost 150 stages. Sets included three miniature Victorian mansions, a 12.8 m apple orchard, and a model of Ashland, Oregon, which included tiny details like Oregon Shakespeare Festival banners. More than twenty-eight animators worked at a time, rehearsing, or filming scenes, producing 90-100 seconds of finished animation each week.

    Puppets were used with the remarkable ability to change their facial expressions, something that would become an emblem of Laika. Each puppet was meticulously designed with removable sections for the top and bottom of the head, allowing for interchangeability of these parts. This ingenious technique allowed the film's characters to exhibit an astonishing variety of more than 208,000 unique facial expressions. This attention to detail in the creation of the puppets greatly contributed to the richness of emotions and nuances presented throughout the film.
